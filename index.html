School Team Qualifies For Finals

By Nabil Almofti | December 16, 2025

San Diego School football team celebrating after qualifying for the finals.

San Diego School, California — The San Diego School football team secured a spot in the state finals by winning 22-14 against Riverside High School at San Diego School Stadium on Sunday, December 14th, making school history.

In a game where the air was tense with excitement, the San Diego Wolves dominated both offensively and defensively. Quarterback Abbas Absi led the charge with two touchdown passes, while center Ray Alrifaey contributed a powerful 110-yard run, sealing the win for the Wolves.

Coach Moe Kousie praised the team's effort, saying, "The players showed incredible focus, teamwork, and form today. This victory is the result of months of hard work, dedication, and training."

Fans packed the stands, cheering loudly throughout the match. Wolve's fan Zayd Sinsikriti reflected on the match: "We won by eight points, and Ray really upped his game this time. Riverside was a tough match, but now we’re looking ahead to the final game next week.

The Wolves will play Valley High School in the state finals next weekend. The team is rigorously preparing for the championship game.

With a spot in the finals secured, the San Diego School football team will compete in a game that will determine the state champion.
